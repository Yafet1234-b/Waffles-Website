Instructions

Preheat the waffle iron.

Turn on your waffle iron and let it heat up completely according to the manufacturer's instructions. Most models have an indicator light that signals when they are ready.

Mix dry ingredients.

In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

Mix wet ingredients.

In a separate medium bowl, whisk the eggs, then add the milk, oil or melted butter, and vanilla extract (if using). Whisk until well combined.

Combine wet and dry.

Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Stir just until combined; a few lumps are okay.

Be careful not to overmix the batter, as this can result in tough, chewy waffles.

Cook the waffles. Lightly grease the preheated waffle iron with cooking spray or a little extra oil. Pour the appropriate amount of batter (check your waffle iron's manual for the exact amount, usually 1/2 to 3/4 cup) onto the hot iron and close the lid.

Cook until golden. Cook the waffles until they are golden brown and crisp, typically for 3 to 5 minutes. A common indicator that a waffle is done is when the iron stops steaming.

Serve. Gently remove the cooked waffles using a fork or tongs and place them on a wire rack to prevent them from getting soggy. Serve immediately with butter, maple syrup, or your favorite toppings.

It is time to dig in!!!
